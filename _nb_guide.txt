PROMPT 5 — Risk-adjusted final ranking

SECTION 6 — Tips, Objection Handling & Known Limitations
Prompt engineering tips for better results
Be specific about the file and sheet: 'From the SLA and Support sheet in VendorC_Ramco_RFP_Response.xlsx...' — this eliminates ambiguity across 8 files
Ask for citations: 'Quote the exact requirement ID and vendor response text that supports this conclusion' — makes output verifiable and trustworthy
Iterate — don't accept the first response: 'You rated Vendor A as PC on ATS-14. Read their response again and tell me if that rating should actually be NC given they mentioned requiring a third-party module.'
Reframe for different audiences: 'Rewrite your recommendation as a 5-bullet point email for a non-technical HR Director audience'
Cross-examine the model: 'You have ranked Vendor F highest. Play devil's advocate — what is the strongest case against choosing Vendor F?'
Build incrementally: start with 3 vendors, get the response, then: 'Now repeat the same analysis adding the other 3 vendors'
Handling common audience objections during the demo
Objection: 'Can Copilot make mistakes? Can we trust this output?'
Response: 'Every output Copilot produces here is grounded only in the documents you uploaded — it doesn't make things up from the internet. It can only say what the vendors actually wrote. What it adds is the ability to synthesise that across 8 files simultaneously and apply your scoring criteria consistently. The job of the procurement team is to verify the top findings — and Copilot has already pointed them to exactly where to look, with the specific requirement ID and quoted text.'
Objection: 'This is just summarising — surely our team can do this already.'
Response: 'Your team can produce summaries. What takes time is the cross-vendor analysis — looking at one requirement across 146 rows in 6 vendor files and spotting where vendor A self-reported FC but their response text says they need a third-party integration. That forensic cross-referencing, done manually, is 3 weeks of work for a team of four. Copilot just did it in 45 seconds.'
Objection: 'What about confidentiality? Vendor proposals are sensitive.'
Response: 'Copilot Notebook is hosted within Microsoft 365 — it does not share your data with anyone, does not use it to train models, and is protected by your organisation's Microsoft 365 data boundary. The files you upload stay in your tenant. This is the same compliance posture as your Teams messages and SharePoint files.'
Known limitations
Limitation 1 — Colours not read:  Copilot reads cell values, not formatting. Our Excel files use text codes (FC/PC/NC/R) in the Compliance Status column — not just cell colours — so this is not an issue for this demo.
Limitation 2 — Context window on very large queries:  Prompts asking Copilot to analyse all sheets of all 6 vendor Excel files simultaneously may produce incomplete responses. Break into phases: 'Analyse vendors A, B, C first. Then D, E, F. Then compare your findings.'
Limitation 3 — No live internet access:  Copilot Notebook is grounded only in your uploaded files. It cannot verify current vendor pricing or certification status. Present this as a BENEFIT: 'The output is 100% based on what the vendors submitted — no hallucinated information from the web.'
Limitation 4 — File upload availability:  Test uploads the day before the demo. If direct upload is unavailable in the tenant, use the OneDrive link method in Section 2, Step 2. Have a backup screen recording of the prompts if all else fails.
